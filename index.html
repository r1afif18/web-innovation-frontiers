Unlocking Indonesia's Data Potential

This application explores four "anti-mainstream" problem areas hidden within Indonesia, where data science can drive significant social and economic impact. Explore each frontier to understand the unique challenges, data landscapes, and proposed innovative project blueprints.

Frontier 1: The Digital Underbelly

Mapping and Mitigating Sophisticated Online Fraud Networks

The problem of online fraud in Indonesia has evolved from single incidents into an organized cybercrime ecosystem. An innovative data science approach must shift from reactive detection to proactive network mapping, uncovering syndicate structures, and understanding the linguistic narratives they use to ensnare victims. This section explores how data from public forums and crowdsourced reports can be turned into actionable intelligence.

Blueprint 1: Fraud Syndicate Mapping

Using Social Network Analysis (SNA) to proactively identify clusters of accounts and phone numbers controlled by the same syndicates, moving from reactive blacklisting to proactive mitigation.

Blueprint 2: Linguistic Fingerprinting of Scams

Building an NLP model to detect the narrative patterns and linguistic style of scams, creating a defense system that is adaptive to new, previously unseen scam variants.

Frontier 2: The Archipelago's Food Arteries

Optimizing Hyper-Local Agricultural Supply Chains

Indonesia's crucial agricultural sector is crippled by a "first-mile data void"—a lack of reliable information on production, quality, and logistics at the farmer level. Rather than a generic solution, this project focuses on modeling a single specific commodity (like cassava) by fusing satellite, weather, and infrastructure data to create actionable intelligence, reducing inefficiency and improving farmer welfare.

Frontier 3: The Fading Voices

A Data-Driven Approach to Revitalizing Regional Languages

Indonesia, with over 700 languages, faces a linguistic extinction crisis. This project goes beyond qualitative documentation by proposing a quantitative approach: measuring a language's "digital vitality." By analyzing a language's digital footprint on the web and social media, we can create an index to prioritize preservation efforts and develop low-resource NLP tools that catalyze revitalization.

Blueprint: Digital Language Vitality Index (DVI)

This index ranks regional languages not just by speaker count, but by their "digital health." Interact with the chart to see how scores change based on different metrics.

Frontier 4: The Silent Epidemic

Quantifying Mental Health Stigma and Access Barriers

Deep-seated cultural stigma prevents many Indonesians from seeking mental health support. Since honest conversations often happen anonymously online, these platforms become a rich data source. This project aims to "diagnose society" by analyzing public discourse to map the landscape of stigma, identify access barriers, and inform culturally and geographically relevant public health interventions.

Blueprint: Mental Health Discourse Dashboard

Dynamically mapping discussion topics and stigma levels across Indonesia to help policymakers allocate resources and design more effective campaigns. Select a region to see a simulation of differing discussion focuses.
